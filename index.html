Getting Into The Mood

Chan, Chan, & Ching

Hey there, sexy…

I want you to sit down and relax… How about a bit of roleplay?

I want you to close your eyes... … and imagine you're a multi-talented but faded music artist wanting to start anew. You’ve peacefully resigned a few decades ago and made your peace long ago, but every now and then, you wonder what could’ve been. You dream of stepping back onto the stage and hear the crowds scream your name.
Aaaaah Aaaaaah.
Just the thought of it fills you with a surge of adrenaline.

And now, after 20 years later, you’ve steeled your resolve and decided that
Yes! I will hide in the shadows no more. Get ready, world. Here I come.
But first, you gotta research. Good for you, information is now just a few clicks away. You can hear it now. Those screams are almost within your reach. But you want to be careful. Don’t want anyone stealing your limelight now, do you?

Let’s start with a few questions you want to get answered first. You know what they say, Knowing your battle is half the battle already. So you start with the questions you want answers to:

1. What is the best genre to make a comeback? Considering the reviews of the people, what genre should you specialise in so that you will not flop in your comeback due to fierce competition?

2. What record label produces the best song according to the best genre? If you wish to have a more receptive audience, having the appropriate record label would not hurt. Assuming you plan to sign a record label, which one would suit your needs the most?

3. What month of the year is it best to release a song of the best genre? Since in the music industry, albums and songs are being released every month, what would be the ideal time for you to quickly gain traction despite the oversaturated music industry?
